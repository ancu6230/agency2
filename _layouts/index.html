What DHARPA is doing

The Digital History Advanced Research Projects Accelerator (DHARPA) is an interdisciplinary team working to enable critical DH practice. We want to help scholars lift the lid on ‘black box’ technologies, to take ownership of their interventions, and to embrace their role in the process of knowledge production.

Software & Methodologies

User-friendly techniques and tools to operationalize self-reflexive, reproducible scholarship.

Kiara

Shine a light on critical digital history practice.

Our interactive software will cultivate the holistic practice of interweaving data, code, computational functionality, and metadata with a narrative of historian’s choices and actions. Documenting academic labour makes its value evident, while also making it reproducible and keeping it honest: it allows scholars to take ownership of their interventions.
Our software will be free and open-source and backed by a long-term sustainability plan and training opportunities to encourage widespread and confident adoption.

Built for both humanities scholars and social scientists, we plan to include modules for:

  • Data ingestion
  • Data standardisation
  • Textual analysis
  • Network analysis
  • Geographical analysis

…and bring them within a seamless environment, where work can flow between tasks from experimentation and modelling to presentation and dissemination.

  • Date: since October 2019
  • Category: Software

Internal Collaborations

Working with our colleagues in the C2DH.

DHARPA is based within the Centre for Contemporary and Digital History at the University of Luxembourg . The C2DH is an interdisciplinary centre founded on the principle that the best scholarship is produced when theory and methods work in tandem.

Collaborations with our local colleagues at the C2DH will be central to our mission.
